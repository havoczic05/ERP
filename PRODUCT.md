# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Two roles inside a single-company electronics-components importer:

- **Administrador** — runs the business: inventory, users, company settings, and the analytics dashboard. Needs financial visibility and control.
- **Vendedor** — front-line sales: creates quotations and sales, registers payments (amortizations), checks stock.

Context: desktop, office, **daily operational use** — often hours at a time entering documents and reviewing receivables. They are not browsing; they are working. Speed of scanning and accuracy matter more than visual novelty.

## Product Purpose

Centralize the three operational pillars — inventory, the sales/quotation cycle, and installment-based accounts receivable — replacing dispersed spreadsheets. Success looks like: fast, accurate daily data entry; fewer stock/credit errors; and clear financial visibility (receivables, overdue, stock risk) at a glance. Money is handled here, so the system must read as reliable and precise.

## Brand Personality

Sober, calm, precise, trustworthy. A focused workhorse, not a showpiece. Voice in the UI is clear and direct; domain terms stay in their business language (cotización, venta, RUC, almacén). The feeling target is "a well-organized desk," not "a flashy dashboard."

## Anti-references

- **Raw Excel / spreadsheet** — hostile black gridlines everywhere, unaligned numbers, zero hierarchy. Functional but punishing.
- **Flat soulless gray / government form** — corporate gray mush with no hierarchy, nothing to anchor the eye, lifeless.

Sober does NOT mean gray-and-dull: the design must feel calm and intentional, with real hierarchy and warmth carried by type, spacing, and restrained accent — not by an absence of design.

## Design Principles

1. **Clarity over decoration.** Every element earns its place. Reduce cognitive load for people who stare at this 8 hours a day.
2. **Numbers are first-class.** Financial and quantitative data must be scannable, right-aligned, tabular, and unambiguous. Tables are core UI, not an afterthought.
3. **Calm, not lifeless.** Restraint and hierarchy create focus; the antidote to "flat gray" is structure and rhythm, not more color.
4. **Color carries meaning.** Accent and state colors map to domain states (confirmada/anulada, cuota vencida, bajo stock) — semantic, never decorative. Color is never the only signal (pair with text/icon) for accessibility.
5. **Trust through precision.** Consistency, alignment, and accuracy signal a system you can trust with money.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG **AA**: body text ≥4.5:1 contrast, large/bold text ≥3:1. Fully keyboard-navigable forms and tables. State is never conveyed by color alone — always paired with a label or icon. Respect `prefers-reduced-motion`. Numeric/tabular data uses tabular figures for alignment.
